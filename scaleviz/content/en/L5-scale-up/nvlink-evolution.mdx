# NVLink Evolution: From 1.0 to 5.0

In the era of large AI models, a single GPU is far from sufficient. How to make
multiple GPUs within one node collaborate efficiently is the core challenge of
Scale-Up. NVIDIA's answer is **NVLink**.

## Why NVLink?

Traditional PCIe bus bandwidth is limited:

- PCIe 3.0 x16: ~32 GB/s (bidirectional)
- PCIe 4.0 x16: ~64 GB/s (bidirectional)
- PCIe 5.0 x16: ~128 GB/s (bidirectional)

In multi-GPU training, collective communication operations like AllReduce need
to exchange large amounts of gradient data between GPUs. PCIe often becomes the
bottleneck. NVLink was designed specifically to solve this problem.

## NVLink Generations

| Gen | First Architecture | Per-link BW | Links | Total BW |
|-----|-------------------|-------------|-------|----------|
| NVLink 1.0 | Pascal (P100) | 20 GB/s | 4 | 160 GB/s |
| NVLink 2.0 | Volta (V100) | 25 GB/s | 6 | 300 GB/s |
| NVLink 3.0 | Ampere (A100) | 25 GB/s | 12 | 600 GB/s |
| NVLink 4.0 | Hopper (H100) | 25 GB/s | 18 | 900 GB/s |
| NVLink 5.0 | Blackwell (B200) | 50 GB/s | 18 | 1800 GB/s |

> From P100 to B200, NVLink total bandwidth grew **11×**.

## Summary

NVLink is the cornerstone of NVIDIA's Scale-Up strategy.
Understanding NVLink's bandwidth, topology, and limitations
is the first step toward understanding modern AI infrastructure.
